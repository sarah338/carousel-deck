CAROUSEL | Interview Guide — Patient discovery
Thank you for making time. I'm doing research to understand what the experience of going through egg freezing or IVF is actually like — not the clinical side, but everything around it. There are no right or wrong
answers. The most valuable thing you can tell me is the truth.
"Tell me about your egg freezing / IVF experience. Walk me through it — from the moment you decided to do it to where you are now." ▶ Just listen. Note where energy shifts.
"What was harder than you expected?"
"What was easier than you expected?"
"When you got lab results during your cycle — how did you find out about them, and what did you do with that information?"
"Were there moments where you felt like you didn't fully understand what was happening in your body or what your results meant?" ▶ If yes: "Tell me about one of those moments. What did you do?"
"Where did you go when you had questions between appointments?" ▶ Note exactly: Google, Reddit, Facebook, called clinic, partner, nothing.
"How would you describe the emotional experience — not the clinical side, just what it felt like?"
"Were there moments where you felt alone in this process?" ▶ If yes: "What did that feel like? What would have helped?"
"What support did you have — from your clinic, from people in your life, from apps or communities?"
"Was there anything you needed emotionally or informationally that you just couldn't find?"
"How would you describe your relationship with your clinic during the process?"
"How did you communicate with your clinic between appointments? How did that feel?"
"After your retrieval or transfer — what happened to your relationship with the clinic?"
"Did you use any apps, platforms, or tools to help you manage the process? What did you like or not like about them?"
"If someone had built a platform specifically designed for women going through this — what would you want it to do?" ▶ Do NOT suggest features. Let them answer freely.
"Is there anything about your experience I haven't asked about that you think is important?"
"If you could change one thing about the experience — just one — what would it be?"
GREEN: Spontaneous Google/Reddit mention, emotional isolation, portal frustration, wishing for more support.
YELLOW: "It was fine" — probe deeper. "My clinic was great" — ask what was still hard.
RED: Genuinely good experience with no gaps across multiple women.

This is not a nice-to-have. A clean, native, asynchronous

Alto Pharmacy — clean, educational, human touches, no login
required.
FINDING 2 — Real-time questions go unanswered for hours
or days. (6/7)
Ilana asked about drinking at a wedding and got the answer three days after the

Maeve's first and most critical use case is instant clinical triage.
Answer the 80% of questions that don't require a physician

FINDING 3 — Instructions are verbal, fragmented, and

Frost's first feature is a single source of truth for all cycle instructions.
Written, visual, asynchronous, always accessible, no login friction.
What to do today. What to expect tomorrow. Why.
